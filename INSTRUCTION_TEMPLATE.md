## 🎯 Core Principles

### 1. **No Repetition Rule**
- **NEVER** repeat vocabulary words across any days
- **NEVER** repeat dialogue scenarios or themes
- **NEVER** reuse the same daily focus topics
- Each day must feel completely unique and fresh

### 2. **Progressive Difficulty**
- **Days 1–60:** Advanced foundational communication
  - Focus: Clear expression, basic professional scenarios, everyday interactions
  - Vocabulary: Accessible but sophisticated
  - Scenarios: Common workplace and social situations

- **Days 61–180:** Professional and strategic communication
  - Focus: Strategic thinking, persuasion, complex problem-solving
  - Vocabulary: Business-oriented, analytical terms
  - Scenarios: Leadership, negotiations, project management

- **Days 181–300:** Persuasive and nuanced communication
  - Focus: Influence, diplomacy, subtle communication
  - Vocabulary: Sophisticated, context-dependent language
  - Scenarios: High-stakes conversations, conflict resolution

- **Days 301–365:** Executive-level articulation and thought leadership
  - Focus: Vision-setting, inspiring others, complex analysis
  - Vocabulary: Thought leadership, innovation, transformation
  - Scenarios: C-suite communication, public speaking, mentoring

### 3. **British English Standards**
- Use British spelling: organisation, prioritise, colour, analyse, programme
- Use British expressions and idioms naturally
- Reflect British professional culture and communication style
- Avoid Americanisms

### 4. **Real-World Applicability**
- All vocabulary must be usable in actual professional conversations
- Scenarios must reflect genuine UK workplace situations
- Avoid textbook or artificial language
- Prioritize spoken English over academic writing

## 📐 Daily Lesson Structure

Each day **MUST** include all of the following sections in this exact order:

---

### **1. Day Header**
```markdown
# Day X: [Theme Title]

## Theme
**[One-line theme description]** – [Expanded explanation of what the learner will focus on]
```

**Requirements:**
- Theme must be unique and never repeated
- Should relate to real daily communication scenarios
- Must be specific and actionable

---

### **2. Advanced Vocabulary**
```markdown
## Advanced Vocabulary

1. **Word** (part of speech) – definition
   - *Example: "Sentence using the word naturally."*

[Repeat for 10 words total]
```

**Requirements:**
- Exactly **10 words** per day
- Include part of speech (noun, verb, adjective, etc.)
- Provide clear, concise definition
- Include realistic example sentence
- Words must be:
  - Advanced but usable
  - Relevant to the day's theme
  - Never repeated across 365 days
  - Common in educated British English

---

### **3. British Expressions**
```markdown
## British Expressions

- **"Expression"** – meaning/explanation
[Repeat for 10-12 expressions]
```

**Requirements:**
- 10–12 British expressions per day
- Must be authentic UK expressions
- Include clear explanations
- Should be usable in professional or social contexts
- Examples: "spot on," "fair enough," "I reckon," "good shout"

---

### **4. Extended Dialogue Practice**
```markdown
## Extended Dialogue Practice

### Conversation 1: [Setting/Context Title]

**Setting: [Time, Location, Context]**

**Speaker A:** Dialogue line

**Speaker B:** Dialogue line

[Continue for 15-25 exchanges]

---

### Conversation 2: [Different Setting/Context]

[Repeat structure]

---

### Conversation 3: [Third Setting/Context]

[Repeat structure]
```

**Requirements:**
- **3 different conversations** per day
- Each conversation: 15–25 exchanges minimum
- Settings must vary (morning planning, meetings, informal chats, etc.)
- Must incorporate:
  - At least 5 vocabulary words from the day's list
  - At least 5 British expressions
  - Natural, realistic dialogue
  - Professional or social scenarios relevant to theme
  - **Movie punchlines or references** (when appropriate) to make conversations more engaging and memorable
- Show progression through a typical day when possible
- Use famous British or international film quotes naturally in context to add interest

---

### **5. Essays**

```markdown
**Word count: ~450 words**

[Essay content - 5-6 paragraphs]

---

## Essay 2: [Different Angle on Theme]

**Word count: ~400 words**

[Essay content - 4-5 paragraphs]
```

**Requirements:**
- **2 essays** per day
- Essay 1: ~450 words (5-6 paragraphs)
- Essay 2: ~400 words (4-5 paragraphs)
- Must incorporate:
  - At least 7 vocabulary words from the day's list

- Should feel like thought leadership pieces

## 💡 Tips for Content Creation

1. **Start with the theme** – Let it guide all other content
2. **Choose vocabulary first** – Ensure words fit the theme and haven't been used
3. **Build dialogues around real scenarios** – Think about actual workplace situations
4. **Write essays conversationally** – Use "I reckon," "we," and personal examples
5. **Make exercises practical** – Learners should be able to apply immediately
6. **Vary the tone** – Mix formal and informal, serious and light-hearted
7. **Include humor when appropriate** – British wit and understatement
8. **Reference current professional trends** – Remote work, digital communication, etc.
9. **Show cultural nuances** – British politeness, indirectness, understatement
10. **Use movie punchlines strategically** – Incorporate famous film quotes naturally to make dialogues memorable and engaging
11. **End positively** – Encourage and motivate the learner
