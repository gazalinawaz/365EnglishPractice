## Vocabulary Focus (14 words)

1. **persuade** (v.) - cause to do through reasoning
   - *I need to persuade the board to approve this investment.*
   - Replace: convince → persuade

2. **compel** (v.) - force or oblige to do something
   - *The data compels us to reconsider our strategy.*
   - Replace: force → compel

3. **influence** (v.) - have effect on character or behavior
   - *Her presentation influenced the committee's decision.*
   - Replace: affect → influence

4. **advocate** (v.) - publicly recommend or support
   - *I advocate for this approach based on evidence.*
   - Replace: support → advocate

5. **champion** (v.) - vigorously support or defend
   - *She champions innovative solutions to complex problems.*
   - Replace: support → champion

6. **articulate** (v.) - express idea clearly and effectively
   - *He articulated the vision brilliantly to stakeholders.*
   - Replace: express → articulate

7. **substantiate** (v.) - provide evidence to support
   - *We must substantiate our claims with solid data.*
   - Replace: prove → substantiate

8. **reinforce** (v.) - strengthen or support
   - *These examples reinforce my central argument.*
   - Replace: support → reinforce

9. **underscore** (v.) - emphasize or highlight
   - *I want to underscore the urgency of this matter.*
   - Replace: emphasize → underscore

10. **resonate** (v.) - evoke images or emotions
    - *This message will resonate with our target audience.*
    - Replace: connect with → resonate

11. **galvanize** (v.) - shock or excite into action
    - *We need to galvanize support for this initiative.*
    - Replace: motivate → galvanize

12. **inspire** (v.) - fill with urge to do something
    - *Her words inspired the team to exceed expectations.*
    - Replace: motivate → inspire

13. **captivate** (v.) - attract and hold interest
    - *His storytelling captivated the entire audience.*
    - Replace: engage → captivate

14. **convince** (v.) - cause to believe firmly in truth
    - *I'm confident we can convince them of the merits.*
    - Replace: persuade → convince

## Persuasive Techniques

### Rhetorical Strategies
1. **Ethos** - Establish credibility
2. **Pathos** - Appeal to emotions
3. **Logos** - Use logical arguments

### Key Persuasive Phrases
- "The evidence compels us to..."
- "I advocate for this because..."
- "This will resonate with stakeholders..."
- "Let me substantiate this claim..."
