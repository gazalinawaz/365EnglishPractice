## Vocabulary Focus (14 words)

1. **captivate** (v.) - attract and hold interest
   - *She captivated the audience with her opening story.*
   - Replace: engage → captivate

2. **engage** (v.) - occupy or attract interest
   - *I'll engage the audience through interactive elements.*
   - Replace: involve → engage

3. **articulate** (v.) - express fluently and coherently
   - *He articulated complex concepts with remarkable clarity.*
   - Replace: express → articulate

4. **emphasize** (v.) - give special importance to
   - *I want to emphasize three critical points today.*
   - Replace: stress → emphasize

5. **illustrate** (v.) - explain by using examples
   - *Let me illustrate this principle with a case study.*
   - Replace: show → illustrate

6. **demonstrate** (v.) - clearly show existence or truth
   - *I'll demonstrate how this solution works in practice.*
   - Replace: show → demonstrate

7. **convey** (v.) - communicate or make known
   - *My goal is to convey the urgency of this initiative.*
   - Replace: communicate → convey

8. **inspire** (v.) - fill with urge to do something
   - *Her presentation inspired us to take immediate action.*
   - Replace: motivate → inspire

9. **persuade** (v.) - cause to do through reasoning
   - *I aim to persuade you of this strategy's merits.*
   - Replace: convince → persuade

10. **resonate** (v.) - evoke images or emotions
    - *This message will resonate with our stakeholders.*
    - Replace: connect with → resonate

11. **deliver** (v.) - present or provide
    - *She delivered a powerful keynote address.*
    - Replace: give → deliver

12. **project** (v.) - present image to others
    - *Project confidence through your voice and posture.*
    - Replace: show → project

13. **command** (v.) - dominate from superior position
    - *He commands attention the moment he speaks.*
    - Replace: control → command

14. **conclude** (v.) - bring to an end
    - *I'll conclude with a call to action.*
    - Replace: end → conclude

---

## Presentation Structure

### Opening
- Captivate attention immediately
- Establish credibility
- Preview key messages

### Body
- Illustrate points with examples
- Demonstrate concepts clearly
- Emphasize critical information

### Closing
- Conclude with impact
- Inspire action
- Resonate emotionally
