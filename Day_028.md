## Vocabulary Focus (14 words)

1. **compose** (v.) - write or create
   - *I need to compose a formal response to the inquiry.*
   - Replace: write → compose

2. **draft** (v.) - prepare preliminary version
   - *Please draft a proposal for the client.*
   - Replace: write → draft

3. **convey** (v.) - communicate message or information
   - *This email should convey our appreciation clearly.*
   - Replace: communicate → convey

4. **acknowledge** (v.) - confirm receipt or recognize
   - *I'm writing to acknowledge receipt of your proposal.*
   - Replace: confirm → acknowledge

5. **clarify** (v.) - make statement less confused
   - *I'm writing to clarify the project timeline.*
   - Replace: explain → clarify

6. **confirm** (v.) - establish truth or correctness
   - *I'd like to confirm our meeting for Tuesday.*
   - Replace: verify → confirm

7. **request** (v.) - politely ask for
   - *I'm writing to request an extension on the deadline.*
   - Replace: ask for → request

8. **inquire** (v.) - ask for information
   - *I'm writing to inquire about the position.*
   - Replace: ask → inquire

9. **propose** (v.) - put forward for consideration
   - *I'd like to propose an alternative approach.*
   - Replace: suggest → propose

10. **recommend** (v.) - put forward as suitable
    - *I recommend we schedule a follow-up meeting.*
    - Replace: suggest → recommend

11. **advise** (v.) - offer suggestions about best course
    - *I'm writing to advise you of the policy change.*
    - Replace: inform → advise

12. **notify** (v.) - inform someone of something
    - *This is to notify you of the upcoming maintenance.*
    - Replace: tell → notify

13. **elaborate** (v.) - develop or present in detail
    - *Could you elaborate on your requirements?*
    - Replace: explain more → elaborate

14. **summarize** (v.) - give brief statement of main points
    - *Let me summarize the key decisions from our meeting.*
    - Replace: recap → summarize

## Email Structure

### Professional Email Format
**Subject Line:** Clear and specific
**Greeting:** Appropriate formality level
**Opening:** State purpose immediately
**Body:** Organize logically with paragraphs
**Closing:** Clear next steps or call-to-action
**Sign-off:** Professional and appropriate

### Key Email Phrases
- "I'm writing to inquire about..."
- "I'd like to request..."
- "Could you please clarify..."
- "I recommend we..."
- "Let me summarize..."
